I am a biostatistics researcher interested in causal inference, statistical learning, and trustworthy AI, with an emphasis on developing rigorous and scalable methodologies for genetics, genomics, biomedical, and environmental data.

I received my Ph.D. in Quantitative and Computational Biology with a Graduate Certificate in Statistics and Machine Learning from Princeton University, where I was advised by Professor John D. Storey. Before Princeton, I received a Master of Science from the Department of Biostatistics at Harvard University in 2019, and received Bachelor of Science and Bachelor of Economics from Peking University in 2017.
